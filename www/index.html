Who are we?

We are a group of amateur, professional, student, and hobbyist biologists who aim to revolutionize the way biological science is used in society.

In the near term, we are setting up a wetlab space. We have growing list of projects we are interested in persuing, which can be found on our Trello Projects Page.

In the long-term, we aim to provide a space where anybody with an idea about biology can safely come and test it out.

Education

Molecular biology 101 (in development)

A 10-session overview of the fundamentals of molecular biology, designed by a McGill PhD student.

Designed to take biology beginners to an intermediate-advanced understanding of molecular biology quickly, this course covers the function of DNA and several biotechnological tools.

This course is still in development. We plan to start offering sessions in the summer of 2014.

Bricobrew

Bricobrew - a biology-focused workshop on fermented foods.

A 3-session workshop in which we prepare a (different set of) beer(s) (every time). We also discuss the biology of fermentation and take a hands-on tour of the world's fermented foods.

Genomikon Workshop

We held our first Genomikon workshop in the fall of 2013. See thisblog postat Synbiota to find out what we did!

About us

Bricobio is a DIYbio / biohacking group located in Montreal. Our members are working on several projects, which can be found in theProjects section.

Getting involved with Bricobio

To join or start a project, visit the project's Synbiotanotebook or post in theBricobio discussion forum, and jump right in! We have a great network of talented people to help you find what you need to bring your ideas to life, and learn what you need to know to get your project off the ground.
